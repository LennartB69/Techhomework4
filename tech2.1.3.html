What is FSC timber and what standards for?

FSC the Forest Stewardship Council which is an non-profit organization dedicated to promoting a environmentally appropriate, beneficial to all partys, contributing to the upholding of forests for future generations. So FSC timber refers to wood and wood products that have been certified by them. This is done through a set of standards that must be met, these include.

1. Must comply with all relevant laws and regulations

2. Must protect areas such as rare and endangered species, and cultural heritage sites.

3. Must respect the rights of indigenous peoples and their land.

4. Must maintain the ecological functions provided by forests, such as soil and wate and habitat preservation.

5. Must provide fair wages and working conditions for forest workers
